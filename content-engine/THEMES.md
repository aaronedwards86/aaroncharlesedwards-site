# Themes, Facts & Approved Links

The editorial map for the Insights section. Every article belongs to exactly one pillar
(it may touch others). Every fact in an article traces to the facts bank below or to a
source verified live during the writing session.

## Theme pillars

### 1. Brandformance & Strategy
The flagship thesis: brand and performance are the same activity. Frameworks, industry
argument, how the model works in practice.
- Published: *Why Brandformance Is the Future of Agency Strategy*, *What Winning a Cannes
  Lion Taught Me*, *The Great Agency Rebundle*
- Angle guide: always tie creative philosophy to business mechanics. The reader is a CMO
  who must defend budgets to a CFO.

### 2. Agency Leadership & Growth
Running an independent agency with financial discipline: hiring, concentration risk,
margins-as-discipline (never actual figures), leadership infrastructure, retention.
- Published: *Five Times on the Inc. 5000*
- Angle guide: growth is a system, not a sprint. Admit what nearly broke.

### 3. Marketing × Finance
The dual lens: what finance training teaches about creative businesses, portfolio thinking
applied to agencies, the economics of attention.
- Published: *From Barclays to Brandformance*
- Angle guide: finance is the source of judgement, not just a backstory.

### 4. Partnership, People & Culture
The sibling partnership, building leadership beyond the founders, the culture that produces
the work, the Charles Alfred Sealay legacy standard.
- Published: *The Sibling System*
- Angle guide: structure protects relationships, not the other way around.

### 5. AI & the Future of Agency Work
First-person, practitioner-grade: what Aaron is actually building and learning, what AI does
to the agency operating model. Never futurist hand-waving.
- Published: *4 Months Down an AI Engineering Hole*
- Angle guide: report from inside the work. Skepticism about hype, specificity about practice.

### 6. Luxury & Culture
How prestige brands maintain relevance: exclusivity vs. accessibility, cultural credibility,
Super Bowl advertising analysis (seasonal: February, ties to NY1 commentary).
- Published: none yet — open territory.
- Angle guide: draws on the Fortune 50 luxury client work; "Luxury in the Age of Noise" is
  the speaking topic to mine.

## Facts bank (canonical, safe to state)

- The Charles Group: independent creative agency, founded 2011 in New York City by siblings
  Aaron Edwards (CEO) and Samantha Edwards (CCO). Offices: New York, Chicago, London.
- Named after Charles Alfred Sealay, their great-grandfather, born and raised in St. Kitts.
- Aaron: from Manchester, UK; ex-Barclays Capital; Forbes 30 Under 30 nominee (2018);
  guest lecturer at Columbia Business School and NYU; NY1 Spectrum News on-air commentator
  (Super Bowl ad analysis); LP in First Close (a fund of funds).
- Model: Brandformance™ — brand marketing and performance marketing fused, consultative,
  solves the business challenge not just the marketing challenge.
- Recognition: Cannes Lion winner; 5× consecutive Inc. 5000; 2× D&AD Pencils and a Webby
  (WSJ × Netflix "Cocainenomics"); Creative Review Annual.
- Clients (public roster): Cartier, HP (incl. HP × Ferrari), Diageo, Boston Consulting Group,
  BNY Mellon, Roc Nation, IBM, St. Regis, Marriott Bonvoy, Bessemer Trust, The Corcoran Group,
  Aveda, Origins, Birkenstock, Mackage, Russell Athletic.
- Leadership: John Dunleavy, Global Managing Director.
- 2020: budgets froze; cut overhead and physical footprint, kept the team.

## Hard rules (mirror of the site brief — these override everything)

1. No IntellectOS. No AILA Holdings. No Winterbird, C&C, roll-ups, acquisitions, or PE strategy.
2. No internal financials: revenue, margins, EBITDA.
3. No lifestyle or family content beyond the established public record (Samantha as co-founder,
   the Sealay naming story).
4. No "serial entrepreneur" framing.
5. Public-record, proven, impressive on its own — or it does not appear.
