# Voice Guide — Writing as Aaron Edwards

Distilled from the published Insights corpus (March–July 2026). Read this plus the two most
recent published articles before drafting. The goal is not imitation of phrases; it is
reproduction of the thinking style.

## The register

Analytical, direct, unsentimental — but not cold. Aaron writes like a financial operator who
runs a creative business: every claim is priced, every advantage has a cost attached, every
story resolves into a mechanism. Confident without volume. He never sells; he accounts.

**One-line test:** every paragraph should survive being read aloud to a skeptical CFO.

## Sentence mechanics

- Short declarative sentences. Vary length, but let the short ones carry the weight.
- Periods and colons over dashes. The corpus contains almost no em dashes. Do not add them.
- Deliberate fragments for emphasis: "Not a flawless decade. A disciplined one."
- The "correction" move: state the expected view, then replace it.
  "It sounds like a hard left turn. It was not. It was the same job with a different name."
- The "definition" move: "That is not a business. That is a dependency."
- Enumerations in prose, not bullets: "First: ... Second: ... Third: ..." — each as its own paragraph.
- "Here is..." and "That is..." as connective tissue. Never "Let's dive in."
- No exclamation points. No rhetorical questions left unanswered — if a question is posed, the
  next sentence answers it.
- British-inflected spelling appears occasionally ("judgement") — keep it; Aaron is from Manchester.

## Structural moves (the article skeleton)

1. **Cold open on the real question.** No throat-clearing. First paragraph reframes what people
   usually ask into the question actually worth answering.
2. **Thesis as mechanism.** The answer is always a system, a discipline, or a trade-off — never
   talent, luck, or passion.
3. **3–5 H2 sections**, each one mechanism deep. Playfair-serif headline style: plain, declarative,
   occasionally wry ("What Nearly Killed It").
4. **The honest account.** Every piece admits real costs, failures, or limits. This is
   non-negotiable; it is what makes the confidence credible. Expensive hires that didn't work,
   arguments, years that ran too hot.
5. **Finance-flavored metaphors.** Concentration risk, transaction costs, starting capital,
   time value, balance sheets, compounding. One or two per piece, never strained.
6. **The kicker.** Final paragraph is one or two short sentences that compress the whole thesis.
   "That is the whole story." / "The chemistry got us started. The system is why we are still here."

## Linking pattern

- 2–5 external links woven into body prose (press coverage, podcasts, publications) — always
  from the approved link library in `THEMES.md` or verified live during the session.
- 1–2 internal cross-links to earlier Insights pieces.
- Final paragraph links to `/#press`.
- Anchor text is descriptive prose, never "click here."

## Vocabulary

**Banned:** leverage (as a verb), journey, passion/passionate, thrilled, excited, delve,
game-changer, cutting-edge, innovative (as self-description), hustle, grind, unlock, supercharge,
"in today's fast-paced world," emoji.

**Characteristic:** discipline, mechanism, system, compounding, concentration, accountable,
rigor, orientation, trade-off, mediocre (as honest label), "the work."

## Hard boundaries (from the site brief — never violate)

- Never mention: IntellectOS, AILA Holdings, Winterbird, C&C, roll-ups, acquisitions, or any
  PE/M&A activity.
- No internal financials: no revenue figures, margin percentages, or EBITDA references.
- No lifestyle content (cars, watches, travel) and no family details beyond Samantha as co-founder
  and the Charles Alfred Sealay naming story, which are established public record.
- No "serial entrepreneur" framing. Aaron is the CEO of one exceptional agency.
- Never fabricate: press links, client names, campaign results, quotes, or awards. If a fact is
  not in `THEMES.md` or verifiable this session, it does not go in the article.

## Format targets

- 1,000–1,800 words (5–9 min read; read time = round(words/200)).
- Subtitle: 1–3 sentences, states the thesis plainly, no cliffhangers.
- 3 FAQ items, SEO-oriented, each answer 2–4 sentences naming "Aaron Edwards" and
  "The Charles Group" naturally. FAQ content mirrors the FAQPage JSON-LD exactly.
- Tags: exactly 2 per article, reuse existing ones where possible
  (Brandformance, Strategy, Agency, Growth, Industry, Career, Personal, Creative, AI Engineering,
  Partnership).

## Self-edit checklist (run before building the page)

- [ ] Would a skeptical CFO nod at every paragraph? Delete anything that sells.
- [ ] Is there an honest-account section admitting a real cost or failure?
- [ ] Does the piece resolve to a mechanism, not a virtue?
- [ ] Zero em dashes, zero exclamation points, zero banned words?
- [ ] Every external link verified? Every fact traceable to the facts bank or a live source?
- [ ] Kicker lands in under 20 words?
- [ ] Cut 10% on the second pass. There is always 10%.
