# Task: Build My Personal Website — aaronedwards.com

## Objective

Build a polished executive personal website that positions me as a leading voice in luxury marketing and creative strategy. The site should do three things:

1. Showcase The Charles Group's track record — world-class clients, world-class work
2. Establish my domain-level expertise across marketing strategy and finance
3. Convert interest into speaking bookings and high-level professional inquiries

This is an outward-facing credibility piece. Nothing about internal strategy, acquisitions, technology builds, or investment vehicles appears on this site. Everything here is public-record, proven, and impressive on its own.

## Audience

- Fortune 50 CMOs and marketing leaders evaluating whether to work with TCG
- Event organizers and university programs looking for speakers
- Press and media considering me as a commentator on luxury marketing, Super Bowl advertising, brand strategy
- LPs, fund managers, and finance professionals who encounter me through the fund-of-funds close and want to understand who I am

## Tone & Archetype

Polished executive. Confident without being loud. The site should feel like walking into a well-appointed office — everything is there for a reason, nothing is performative.

Reference sites:
- **adambraun.com** — Clean multi-faceted CEO positioning. Speaking-forward. Webflow-built.
- **jamesclear.com** — One-sentence positioning clarity. Lets the work speak.
- **rauchg.com** — Minimalist authority. No noise.

## Site Architecture

### Page 1: Home

**Hero Section:**
- Professional editorial-quality portrait (not a corporate headshot — think photographed for a feature, not an ID badge)
- Positioning line: "Founder & CEO of The Charles Group. Over a decade building luxury brand experiences for the world's most prestigious companies."
- Subline or small text: "Speaker. Investor. Strategist."

**Client Logo Strip:**
- The headline here is the caliber of client. This is the single most important visual on the page.
- Logos (confirm permissions): Cartier, HP, Richemont, and any other Fortune 50 / blue-chip names from the TCG roster
- Keep it to 4-6 logos max. Quality over quantity. Every logo should make someone pause.

**Work Highlights (optional but powerful):**
- 2-3 visual case study cards showing TCG's best work
- Each card: campaign image, client name, one-line result or description
- These don't need full write-ups — just enough to prove the work is real and exceptional
- If we don't have approved visuals ready at launch, skip this section and add it later. An empty portfolio section is worse than none.

**Domain Expertise Block:**
- Short section establishing the two pillars of my expertise:
  - **Luxury Marketing & Brand Strategy** — "Over a decade leading creative strategy for Fortune 50 luxury brands, with deep expertise in how prestige brands build, protect, and extend cultural relevance."
  - **Finance & Investment** — "LP in First Close, a fund of funds, with active positions across equities, real assets, and international real estate. Brings a financial operator's lens to every strategic decision."
- This is NOT a bio. It's a positioning statement. Two short paragraphs, no life story.

**Press & Speaking Strip:**
- "As Seen On" / "Featured In" section
- Logos and/or text: NY1 Spectrum News, Columbia Business School, NYU, Super Bowl LX Coverage
- This section does heavy lifting — it says "other serious institutions have already validated this person"

**CTA:**
- "Book Aaron to speak" → Speaking page
- "Get in touch" → Contact page

## Hard Rules — What Does NOT Go On This Site

1. **No IntellectOS.** Not mentioned, not hinted at. It's an internal build.
2. **No AILA Holdings.** Not mentioned. The real estate activity is separate.
3. **No PE strategy.** No mention of roll-ups, acquisitions, Winterbird, C&C, or any M&A activity.
4. **No internal financials.** No revenue figures, no margin percentages, no EBITDA references. The proof is in the client roster and the longevity.
5. **No "serial entrepreneur" framing.** I'm the CEO of one exceptional agency. That's the story.
6. **No lifestyle content.** No cars, watches, travel, personal interests. Strictly professional.
7. **No blog unless I commit to monthly publishing.** LinkedIn is my content channel. Link to it, don't replicate it.

## Design Direction

- **Platform:** Webflow
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif with strong hierarchy. Inter, Neue Haas Grotesk, or similar.
- **Color:** Restrained. Dark navy or charcoal base, generous white space, one accent color max. Think luxury brand aesthetic — the site should feel like it belongs next to the clients I serve.
- **Photography:** Editorial quality is non-negotiable. Minimum one professional portrait. Ideally a second shot for Speaking page (on stage or in a professional context).
- **Mobile:** Must be flawless. CMOs and event organizers will look at this on their phones.
- **Speed:** Fast. No heavy animations, no auto-playing video, no parallax effects. Respected people don't need gimmicks.
