## Speaker Bio — Long (150 words)

Aaron Edwards is the Founder and CEO of The Charles Group, a Cannes Lions-winning, five-time Inc. 5000 creative agency with offices in New York, Chicago, and London. Over the past decade, he has built the firm into a trusted partner for Fortune 50 brands — leading engagements with Cartier, HP, Diageo, Boston Consulting Group, BNY Mellon, Roc Nation, and others across luxury, lifestyle, and financial services.

Aaron pioneered what he calls "Brandformance™" — the intersection of brand marketing and performance marketing, combined with a consultative approach built on analytical rigor, financial modeling, and forecasting that solves for the business challenge, not just the marketing challenge. It's a differentiated approach rooted in his finance background: every creative decision is informed by data, every campaign is accountable to outcomes. He is an LP in First Close, a fund of funds, and holds positions across equities, real assets, and international real estate.

Aaron is a guest lecturer at Columbia Business School and NYU, and has been featured on NY1 Spectrum News as a commentator on Super Bowl advertising and marketing trends. He is based in New York.

## About Section — Draft

Aaron Edwards founded The Charles Group over a decade ago with a simple thesis: the best creative work comes from agencies that think like businesses, not just studios.

That conviction built a firm that now serves Fortune 50 clients across luxury, lifestyle, and financial services — Cartier, HP, Diageo, Boston Consulting Group, Roc Nation — from offices in New York, Chicago, and London. A five-time Inc. 5000 honoree, the work has earned a Cannes Lion, D&AD Pencils, Webby Awards, and a reputation for creative excellence that moves numbers.

Before building The Charles Group, Aaron studied and worked in finance — a background that still shapes every engagement. Where most agencies lead with creative, Aaron leads with analytical rigor: financial modeling, forecasting, and business-case development that turns marketing from a cost center into a growth engine. He coined the term "Brandformance™" to describe this philosophy — fusing brand strategy with performance accountability to solve for the business challenge, not just the marketing challenge.

He is based in New York.
